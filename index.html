IIM Cancer Screening Assessment Tool

Risk-stratified cancer screening calculator for adults with idiopathic inflammatory myopathy (IIM) — including dermatomyositis, polymyositis, immune-mediated necrotizing myopathy, and anti-synthetase syndrome — implementing the 2023 IMACS international guideline.

Takes about 2–3 minutes to complete. Free. No patient data stored.

About the Calculator

This tool implements the 2023 International Guideline for IIM-Associated Cancer Screening, developed by the International Myositis Assessment and Clinical Studies Group (IMACS) and published in Nature Reviews Rheumatology (Oldroyd et al., 2023).

It uses IIM subtype, autoantibody profile, demographics, and clinical features — together with red-flag symptoms and geographic considerations — to generate a patient-specific risk category (standard, moderate, or high) and an individualised cancer-screening plan.

Tool Limitations

Risk categories are relative to the IIM population, not the general population — standard-risk patients still likely carry elevated risk versus the general population. No source-guideline recommendation has GRADE A (high-quality) evidence; many rest on expert consensus. Negative screening does not exclude cancer.

This tool does not apply to:

  • Juvenile-onset IIM (under 18 at symptom onset) — additional cancer screening is not routinely required (standard risk equivalent)
  • Verified inclusion body myositis (IBM) — additional cancer screening is not routinely required (standard risk equivalent)
  • Patients with currently active or recently diagnosed cancer
  • Patients more than 3 years from IIM symptom onset (recommendations less validated)

All IIM patients should continue age- and sex-appropriate general-population cancer screening regardless of this tool's output.

🔒 No patient data is stored

This tool processes all information locally in your browser. No patient information is transmitted to, collected by, or stored on any external server. When you close or refresh this page, all entered data is permanently erased. Exported files (PDF, text, JSON) are generated in your browser and downloaded directly to your device.

Do not enter directly-identifying patient information (name, date of birth, medical record number) into this tool. Use anonymous case identifiers only if you wish to label an assessment.

What is remembered locally on your device: your selected country and that you have acknowledged these disclaimers. These can be cleared at any time by clearing your browser data. No clinical or patient information is included.

Privacy & data handling

No clinical or patient data is transmitted to or stored on any server. All risk calculations occur locally within your browser. Information you enter exists only in browser working memory and is permanently deleted when you close or refresh the page.

Local preferences (your selected country and confirmation that you have read the disclaimers) are stored in your browser only, on your device, to avoid asking you the same questions on every visit. These can be cleared by clearing your browser data. No clinical or patient information is included in these local preferences.

Exported files (PDF, text, JSON) are generated in your browser and downloaded directly to your device. They are not transmitted through any server.

Because no patient data is stored or transmitted, this tool does not require HIPAA Business Associate Agreements (US), GDPR data processing agreements (EU/UK), or equivalent arrangements in other jurisdictions. However, clinicians remain responsible for handling any exported files in accordance with applicable privacy laws.

EMR interoperability

Exported output is structured to facilitate transfer to electronic medical records (EMRs). JSON export uses HL7 FHIR-aligned concepts (Condition, Observation, RiskAssessment, ServiceRequest, CarePlan) with SNOMED CT and LOINC code mappings where applicable. The clinical-note text export is formatted for copy-paste into Epic, Cerner, EMIS, SystmOne, and similar systems.
